More than 10 million Afghan residents are potentially refugees as the extremist Taliban and other terrorist groups take over their country.

What we do?

We provide a "family support" kit, which is filled with personal hygiene essentials and PPE Covid19 protective products, such as face masks, hand sanitizers, etc. We also provide hygiene and nutrition supplies for babies, toddlers, and mothers. Also, we are buying clothes, blankets and bedding. We pay special attention to mothers and babies, the elderly and others with special health needs.

As the fighting in Afghanistan spreads, thousands more families are fleeing for their lives. An estimated 550,780 people have now been displaced by conflict in Afghanistan so far this year, among whom approximately 60% are children.


How you can help

Help Save Children in Afghanistan

Afghan children have known nothing but conflict their entire lives. Right now, the situation for children in Afghanistan is dire, with at least 75,000 having had to flee their homes in the last month amid escalating violence. Even before the events of the last month, Afghanistan had the second-highest number of people facing emergency levels of hunger in the world.
Save the Children has been helping families in Afghanistan since 1976. There has never been a more important time to affirm our dedication to the Afghan people and our commitment to stay and deliver. As the United States begins welcoming newly arriving Afghan refugees

If you give $36 a month for a year: You could provide an emergency shelter kit, including a family tent and 2 plastic tarpaulins, to a displaced family in Afghanistan

If you give $24 a month for a year: You could provide a kit of core relief items, including blankets, sleeping mats, jerrycans, a bucket, mosquito nets and a kitchen set, to four Afghan families staying in Pakistan

If you give $15 a month for a year: You could provide a sanitary kit (3 bars of laundry soap, 3 bars of toilet soap and 2 boxes of sanitary napkins) to 18 Afghan refugees staying in Iran
